Why SIP Works Like DevOps

  • Automation removes emotional decisions
  • Eliminates market timing
  • Removes FOMO
  • Prevents panic
  • Builds discipline

Rupee Cost Averaging Magic

  • High markets: buy fewer units
  • Low markets: buy more units
  • Long term: averages out volatility
  • Example: ₹10,000 SIP over 12 months, average cost < average NAV

Compound Interest Amplification

  • Early years: small gains
  • Middle years: acceleration
  • Later years: exponential growth

SIP Best Practices

  • Start early and small
  • Annual SIP increases (step-up SIPs)
  • Don’t stop during downturns
  • Goal-based SIP allocation (emergency, short-term, long-term, retirement)

SIP vs Lump Sum: The Deployment Strategy

  • SIP: risk mitigation, discipline, flexibility, matches cash flow
  • Lump sum: market timing, immediate exposure, single transaction
  • Verdict: SIP is better for most due to behavioral and risk advantages

Speaker Notes:

  • SIP = automated, disciplined investing.
  • Rupee cost averaging and compounding are powerful.
  • Start early, increase SIPs, don’t stop in downturns.
  • SIPs are usually better than lump sum for most investors.
