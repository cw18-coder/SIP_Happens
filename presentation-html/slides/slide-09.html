Types of Investment Risk – Common Vulnerabilities

Market Risk (Systematic)

Cannot be diversified

The entire market crashes – like a major cloud provider going down

Impact: Affects all investments
Examples: 2008 financial crisis, COVID-19 market crash
Mitigation: Asset allocation, time diversification

Credit Risk (Default)

Company-specific

Company/issuer defaults – like a major vendor going bankrupt

Primary Effect: Debt funds
Examples: IL&FS crisis, DHFL default
Mitigation: High-rated papers, diversify issuers

Liquidity Risk

Exit constraints

Cannot exit when needed – like being locked into a bad contract

Affects: Small-cap funds, some debt funds
Examples: Franklin Templeton fund closures
Mitigation: Emergency funds in liquid investments

Concentration Risk

Portfolio focus

Too many eggs in one basket – like having only one major client

Types: Stock/sector/geographic concentration
Examples: Only tech stocks, only Indian markets
Mitigation: Diversify across assets, sectors, regions

Interest Rate Risk

Bond price impact

Bond prices fall when rates rise – like server costs increasing

Primary Effect: Debt funds
Examples: RBI rate hikes affecting bond NAVs
Mitigation: Choose appropriate duration

Risk Measurement Tools – Your Risk Analytics Dashboard

Standard Deviation

Measures volatility – like API response time variance

Low (< 10%): Conservative funds
Medium (10-20%): Moderate risk funds
High (> 20%): Aggressive funds

Maximum Drawdown

Worst-case scenario loss from peak to trough

Purpose: Shows downside risk
Benefit: Understand worst-case scenarios
Example: "This fund fell 35% during 2008 crisis"

Value at Risk (VaR)

Potential loss with given confidence level

Example: "95% confidence monthly loss won't exceed 8%"
Use Case: Position sizing
Benefit: Risk budgeting

Speaker Notes:

  • Risk Assessment: Understanding different types of investment risks is crucial for portfolio protection
  • Measurement Tools: Use quantitative metrics like standard deviation and drawdown to evaluate fund risk
  • Diversification: Most risks can be mitigated through proper asset allocation and diversification
  • Time Horizon: Longer investment periods help weather market volatility and systematic risks
