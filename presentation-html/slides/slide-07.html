Key Performance Metrics - Your Investment Dashboard

Alpha (α)

Excess return over benchmark - fund manager's skill

Interpretation: Positive Alpha = Outperforming market
Example: Nifty 50: 12%, Your fund: 15% = +3% Alpha
Good Range: 1-3% for equity funds

Beta (β)

Measures fund's volatility relative to market

Interpretation: Beta > 1 = More volatile than market
Example: Beta 1.2 = 20% more volatile than Nifty
Typical Range: 0.8-1.5 for equity funds

Rolling Returns

Performance consistency across time periods

Interpretation: Consistent rolling returns = Reliable fund
Example: 3-yr rolling: 12%, 15%, 12%, 14% (consistent)
Look For: Low deviation in rolling returns

Sharpe Ratio

Risk-adjusted returns - efficiency measure

Interpretation: Higher Sharpe = Better risk-reward balance
Example: Fund A: Sharpe 1.5 vs Fund B: Sharpe 0.9
Good Range: Above 1.0 for equity funds

Sortino Ratio

Risk-adjusted returns considering only downside volatility

Interpretation: Higher Sortino = Better downside risk management
Example: Fund A: Sortino 1.8 vs Fund B: Sortino 1.2
Advantage: More accurate than Sharpe for volatile markets

Standard Deviation

Measures fund's volatility - risk indicator

Interpretation: Lower SD = More stable returns
Example: Large-cap: 15%, Mid-cap: 25%, Small-cap: 35%
Use Case: Compare risk levels across funds

Debt Fund Metrics - Stability Monitoring System

Debt fund analysis is like monitoring a well-architected system - more predictable patterns with measurable risk factors.

Yield to Maturity (YTM)

Expected annual return if held to maturity

Current Range: 5-8% for most debt funds
Interpretation: Higher YTM = Higher risk/longer duration
Use Case: Compare similar-category funds
Example: Corporate bond fund 7% YTM vs Govt bond fund 6% YTM

Modified Duration

Sensitivity to interest rate changes

Rule of Thumb: 1% rate change = Modified Duration % NAV change
Short Duration: 1-3 years (low rate risk)
Medium Duration: 3-4 years (moderate rate risk)
Long Duration: 7+ years (high rate risk)

Credit Quality Analysis

Reliability of underlying securities

AAA Rated: Highest safety, lowest returns
AA Rated: High safety, slightly higher returns
A Rated & Below: Higher risk, higher potential returns

Universal Metrics - Cross-Platform Analytics

These metrics apply to all fund types and help evaluate cost efficiency and portfolio quality across your investment stack.

Portfolio Turnover Ratio

How frequently holdings change - like deployment frequency

Typical Range: 25-75% annually for equity funds
High Turnover: Active management, higher costs
Low Turnover: Buy-and-hold strategy, lower costs

Expense Ratio

Total cost of ownership - like cloud computing costs

Direct Equity Plans: 0.5-1.5%
Direct Debt Plans: 0.2-0.8%
Impact: 1% higher expense = ₹10,000 less per ₹1L over 10 years

AUM Size & Fund Quality

Fund size affects agility and market impact

Too Small: < ₹100 cr (lack of diversification)
Sweet Spot: ₹500-5,000 cr (optimal balance)
Too Large: > ₹10,000 cr (reduced agility)
